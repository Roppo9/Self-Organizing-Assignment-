Self Discipline

Self-discipline is one’s ability to do what they know is right. One’s level of self-discipline is measured by how well they are able to overcome temptations to do otherwise.



Self-discipline is one of the harder ideas to implement into a business because it is something that is on a person-to-person basis. Not everyone possesses the same amount of self-discipline in a company, so it is something that can only be encouraged by the group leaders and managers. If a member of a group lacks self-discipline, then the work that they do may either be lack luster or received late compared to the other group members.

Leaders in Business

Leaders who possess self-discipline are able to overcome distractions that they face and focus on the task at hand. If a leader is willing to put time and effort into their work, then the employees that work for them, or the other members of the group that they are working in, will be motivated to do the same. If a leader is able to overcome temptations and resist their impulses, everyone else will notice, and focus on the work that they need to get done.

If businesses had leaders without self-discipline, there wouldn’t be any work getting done consistently. This would be due to the fact that the leaders would often get sidetracked since they would not be able to focus on what needs to be done. Their group partners would see this behavior and either do the same or get demotivated and give up on the work that is given to them. Either way, having a leader without self-discipline wouldn’t be good for running a business if that businesses priority is getting work done in a timely and orderly fashion.

Businesses can help improve productivity in the workplace by choosing a leader who can maintain their self-discipline and can encourage their behavior in those who work with them. As long as that leader is able to stay self-disciplined, work will consistently be getting done. Not only will these leaders be able to boost productivity, but they will also improve the atmosphere in the workplace since self-discipline tends to increase self-esteem and work ethic. Those who work with a leader who is determined to get the work done will feel better about the work that they do if they are trying to work as hard as them.

Employees who have a leader who is self-disciplined may still be unable to complete the work that Is given to them if they lack the motivation to get the work done. In this scenario is it best to provide the employee what is expected of them so that they know what they must do. If they know that you want to see improvement in their initiative and problem solving, then it is best to let them know that is what you expect from them. If they know what is important to you and the business that you are running, they will know what the goals that they should have when completing their work.



Rewards/ Communication/ Encouragement

Another way to encourage self-discipline in the people you are working with is to reward them for that hard work that they do. In this situation, it is important to consider what is important to the person you are rewarding since it would motivate them more if they rewarded correctly. These rewards are important because it helps communicate to the person you are working with that you appreciate the hard work they put in, and it also is able to increase or maintain the productivity that they have.

Encouraging new ideas in projects or in the workplace will also encourage group members to get their work done since they know the company will hear out their concerns regardless of the circumstance. If they feel comfortable with sharing ideas and their ideas are heard, they will be more likely to contribute to the company’s goals and needs.

Open communication can also increase productivity since those who you are working with will be more likely to be aware of your goals. If they know what you desire to accomplish, they are more likely to act independently to help reach the goals that you have. They also will be more likely to set goals for themselves that align with what you want to get done.

If people in your group continue to not practice self-discipline, then it is best to address it immediately. If this behavior continues without being corrected, the group’s overall work ethic will suffer since they know lackluster behavior will go unpunished.
